Chapter 1 . Real Estate Law and Real Estate Business
According to Arizona statutes, a real estate broker or salesperson is any person,
partnership, limited liability company, or corporation who for another and for compen-
sation participates in the following:
1. Sells, exchanges, purchases, rents, or leases real estate or time-share interests
2. Offers to sell, exchange, purchase, rent, or lease real estate or time-share interests
3. Negotiates the sale, exchange, purchase, rental, or leasing of real estate or time-share
interests
4. Lists real estate or time-share interests for sale, lease, or exchange
5. Auctions real estate or time-share interests
6. Buys, sells, or otherwise deals in options on real estate or time-share interests or
improvements to real estate or time-share interests
7. Collects rent for the use of real estate or time-share interests
8. Advertises as being engaged in the business of buying, selling, exchanging, renting,
or leasing real estate or time-share interests
9. Assists or directs in the procuring of prospects calculated to result in the sale,
exchange, leasing, or rental of real estate or time-share interests
10. Aassists or directs in the negotiation of any transaction intended to result in the sale,
exchange, leasing, or rental of real estate or time-share interests
11. Negotiates a loan secured or to be secured by a mortgage or other encumbrance upon
transfer of real estate or time-share interests
12. Engages in the business of assisting another in filing an application for the purchase
or lease of lands owned by the state or federal government
13. Claims, charges, receives, or collects an advance fee in connection with any
employment mentioned in this section
14. Performs any ofthe foregoing acts for the sale or lease of other than real property ifa
real property sale or lease is a part of the transaction
15. Performs any ofthe foregoing acts for compensation as an employee of the owner of
real estate.
License Exemption
The requirement for licensing does not apply to the following:
1. Persons, partnerships, limited liability companies, or corporations dealing in their
own property who do not receive special compensation
2. Persons or entity acting for another with power of attorney for a specific purpose in
an isolated transaction and not as a method of conducting a real estate business
3. Attorney at law in his or her normal duties
4. Person acting under a court order, such as a bankruptcy trustee
5. Trustee selling under a deed of trust
6. Person acting as Residential Leasing Agent
7. Any officer or employee ofa governmental agency in the conduct of the officer'sor
employee's official duties
8. Person managing no more than one non-residential income property and employed
by the owner or the owner's licensed management agent
9. Natural person, in the employ of a designated broker, who performs only clerical,
bookkeeping, accounting, and other administrative and support duties
