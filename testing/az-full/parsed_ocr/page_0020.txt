Preface
Arizona Principles of Real Estate, a comprehensive text for students entering the real
estate profession, uniquely combines national and Arizona real estate principles and
practices into one book. It is derived from a text originally written in the 1980s that
has been used in the successful completion of the 90-hour licensing education require-
ments for thousands of Arizona real estate professionals. The authors' vision is to pro-
vide a single text that includes the subjects set forth in the Arizona Department of Real
Estate (ADRE) course outlines for the broker and salesperson prelicensure courses.
Each chapter is consistently organized with the following sections:
Learning Objectives
Provide focus for the reader at the beginning of each chapter
Are measurable and based on the ADRE outlines
Chapter Content
Is presented in the order of the objectives
Is written in a clear and concise style
Follows easy-to-use headings
Presents key terms in boldface print
Chapter Summary
Assists the reader in identifying key chapter concepts
Important Terms and Concepts
Are listed at the end of each chapter
Afford a quick reference of professional terminology
Checking Your Comprehension
Asks questions related to the chapter's leaming objectives
Is provided in discussion and multiple-choice question format
Reviewing Your Understanding
Enables the reader to check understanding of chapter content
Aids the student in preparing for the state examination
Other features include:
A complete glossary
State examination study guide
Practice state examination
Practice real estate mathematics section
The authors believe that the text provides all of the information and study tools neces-
sary to enable a real estate student to pass the State of Arizona licensing examinations.
In addition, the text can help a prospective licensee establish a foundation for a success-
ful career as a real estate professional.
